Part 2

How you fund yourself
as a trader.

It happens in three stages
Beginner→Intermediate→Expert
Stage 1

Beginner

Prop firms only.
So what is a prop firm

You trade their capital.

You pass a test, they fund you, you keep a cut of what you make.
How it works
1Pass the evaluation
2Get funded
3Take payouts
The bits that actually matter
→The buffer
→Trailing drawdown
→How your targets are set
The beginner ladder
3 → 5 → 10 → 20
funded accounts. You scale by adding more, not by risking more.
Stage 2

Intermediate

Stop relying on one firm.
Once one firm is working

Stack across several firms
at once.

More capital, risk spread wider. There's a tool that makes it manageable.
Stage 3

Expert

Prop firms, plus your own money.
When to add personal funds

Once your cash is solid,
start a small personal account.

For example, at 50k in cash, put 5k aside to trade as your own.
The engine
→Take a slice of every payout
→Feed your personal account
→Repeat, and let it grow
Then the risk grows with it
$300 → $1,000 → $1,500
Risk per trade, scaling up only as the account does.
Prop vs personal

Prop firms

  • →Their capital
  • →Their rules
  • →Scale fast

Personal

  • →Your capital
  • →Your rules
  • →Full upside
